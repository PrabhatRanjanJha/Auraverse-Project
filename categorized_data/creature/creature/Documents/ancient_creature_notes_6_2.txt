The creature described in these notes is a magnificent and formidable being from ancient legends. 
It possesses an immense, scaled body that glistens under sunlight like burnished metal. 
Two vast wings extend from its back, capable of creating storms when they beat the air. 
Its breath, according to tales, can melt stone and ignite forests, and many stories speak of villages lost to a single exhalation.

Old scrolls describe its intelligence as almost human, sometimes greater, and it is often said to guard treasures or sacred relics deep within mountains or ruins. 
Its eyes are said to glow like molten gold, and its roar can be heard for miles. 
Some ancient kings sought to tame or slay such a being to prove their valor, while others offered tributes to keep it asleep in its cavern.

Though rarely seen, signs of its presence are said to include scorched earth, claw marks on cliffs, and the smell of sulfur carried by the wind. 
Legends warn that disturbing its rest invites calamity upon entire kingdoms. 
Even the bravest warriors of old whispered its name only in fear and reverence.
